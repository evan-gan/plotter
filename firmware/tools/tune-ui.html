Before you start
  1. Put a pen in the holder with the tip touching taped-down paper.
  2. Hand-move the carriage so there is ≥120 mm of free travel in +X and +Y.
  3. Close anything else using the serial port (a G-code sender, the terminal).

Test shapes & diagnostics

Traces one 20 mm-diameter circle from the current position, then returns to origin.
Same circle at 300 mm/min. Backlash flat-spots show at the four 45° points; if they vanish slow, it's inertia, if they stay, it's belt slack.
Same circle at the board's configured max feedrate ($110) — reflects the real top speed your tuned settings allow.
Four out-and-back strokes from one centre. A doubled (non-overlapping) line = lost motion; the two diagonal strokes isolate which belt.

ETA calibration

Runs a few action types (line, diagonal, dense zigzag, circle, pen lifts) at the board's stored feed, compares the estimate to the real time, and fits correction scalers. Pen stays up — no ink needed. Clear ~80 mm of +X/+Y travel.
